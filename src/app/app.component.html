The Heritage

The Heritage of Athangudi Tiles

Athangudi Tiles are a timeless symbol of South India's rich architectural heritage, originating from the historic Chettinad region of Tamil Nadu. Named after the village of Athangudi in Sivaganga district, these handcrafted tiles have been admired for generations for their vibrant colors, elegant patterns, and exceptional durability.

The art of making Athangudi tiles dates back over a century and continues to be preserved by skilled artisans who follow traditional methods passed down through generations. Unlike modern factory-produced tiles, Athangudi tiles are handcrafted using a unique process where colorful cement mixtures are poured onto glass plates and carefully cured under natural sunlight. This traditional technique creates their signature glossy finish without the need for polishing.

Known for their intricate floral and geometric designs, Athangudi tiles bring a distinctive charm to homes, heritage buildings, hotels, and commercial spaces. Their eco-friendly manufacturing process requires no energy-intensive kilns, making them a sustainable flooring choice. Additionally, these tiles naturally remain cool underfoot, making them ideal for the warm climate of Tamil Nadu.

Today, Athangudi tiles continue to represent the perfect blend of traditional craftsmanship, artistic beauty, sustainability, and durability, preserving the legacy of Chettinad culture while enhancing modern interiors.
